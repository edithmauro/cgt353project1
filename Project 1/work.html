Honeysuckle No More

TECH 120 Course Project

The purpose of this assignment was to find any specific problem that is related to West Lafayette, and create a product that would resolve it.

My group chose to focus on reducing the invasive plant species, Japanese Honey Suckle. This plant is found in many areas on Purdue's campus, and has done much damage to the local wildlife (according to local experts). This species is not only fast-growing, but it is also very hard to contain and destroy without harming the surrounding local flora.

Our solution includes creating a durable, multi-layer plant barrier that both kills the invasive spieces, and allows new plants to grow in order to restore the plant life in that area.

"Food Deserts and Their Effect on the Rate of Obesity"

Published Research

This is a semester-long original research project that is centered around the question, "Does living in a food desert have an effect on the average rate of obesity?" Using data based in Indiana, I was able to conclude that there is, very surprisingly, no correlation between these two variables.

This research was both published in the Journal of Purdue Undergraduate Research Vol. 8, and featured in the Purdue Undergraduate Poster Conference.
